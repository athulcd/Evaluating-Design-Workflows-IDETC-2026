detail_design:
  design_summary: |
    The final design is a manual lever-actuated press (CV3) optimized for workshop use. 
    The Main Frame (B1) is a stiff C-frame weldment that mounts to a workbench via the 
    Base Plate (B2). Force amplification is achieved through a multi-linkage system (B4) 
    providing a mechanical advantage of approximately 4:1, ensuring the 500 N riveting 
    force (R8) is met with only 125 N of hand force (R17). 

    The riveting process uses interchangeable hardened tool steel heads (B5, B6) to 
    accommodate different rivet sizes (R7). The closure distance is precisely controlled 
    by an Adjustable Stroke Stop (B8), which allows the operator to fine-tune the 
    4mm closure target (R9) for different stack thicknesses (R5). 

    The Workholding Fixture (B7) features sliding datums to support the wide range of 
    brake shoe diameters (250-400mm) and widths (80-400mm) required by R2 and R3. 
    Safety is addressed by a transparent Polycarbonate Guard (B10) and ergonomic 
    clearances to prevent pinch hazards (R18).

  assembly_instructions: |
    1. Weld the Main Frame (B1) to the Bench Mounting Plate (B2), ensuring perpendicularity of the head axis.
    2. Install the Linkage Assembly (B4) using Pivot Pins (B9); lubricate all joints with workshop grease.
    3. Press-fit the Lower Riveting Head (B6) into the frame base.
    4. Attach the Actuation Lever (B3) to the linkage.
    5. Install the Adjustable Stroke Stop (B8) and verify the range of motion.
    6. Mount the Workholding Fixture (B7) and align with the riveting axis.
    7. Attach the Safety Guard (B10) using M5 fasteners.
  maintenance_requirements: |
    - Monthly: Lubricate pivot pins (B9) and linkage joints (B4).
    - Quarterly: Inspect riveting heads (B5, B6) for wear or chipping; replace if closure quality degrades.
    - Annual: Check frame weldment (B1) for fatigue cracks and verify mounting bolt tightness.
  assumptions:
  - Standard workshop tools (welder, lathe, mill) are available for fabrication.
  - Rivets used are DIN 7338 as per the functional requirements.

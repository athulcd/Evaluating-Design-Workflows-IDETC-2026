  selected_concept_id: CV3
  selection_rationale: |
    CV3 (Value Versatile Press) is selected because it best balances the stringent cost target (400 CHF) with the functional requirements. It utilizes a toggle linkage (WP6) to ensure the 500 N riveting force is achieved with minimal operator effort (meeting R1 and R7). The sliding radius support (WP1) provides the necessary flexibility for the specified range of brake shoe diameters (R3) without the added cost of multiple mandrels. While it lacks the precision centering of CV1, the recessed die (WP4) is a cost-effective solution that meets the basic alignment needs for DIN 7338 rivets.
  sensitivity_analysis_notes: |
    The selection is sensitive to the actual machining hours required for the toggle linkage. If the linkage proves too complex for the budget, the design might need to revert to a simpler lever, though this would negatively impact operator force (EC2). Additionally, if the workshop finds the manual alignment in the recessed die (WP4) leads to too many rejects, the spring-loaded pilot (WP3) from CV1 could be integrated as a high-precision upgrade, likely pushing the cost closer to the 500 CHF limit.
  assumptions:
    - The toggle linkage can be manufactured using standard workshop milling and drilling without specialized CNC equipment.
    - The 500 N force provided by the toggle is sufficient for the full range of DIN 7338 copper rivets (3-5 mm).
    - Standard steel stock prices remain stable enough to support the 400 CHF target.
  open_questions:
    - Is the tactile feedback from the hard stop (WP9) sufficient for the operator, or is a visual stroke indicator required?
    - Can the sliding support (WP1) be effectively locked using simple hand-tightened knobs to meet the 70-rivet/day pace?

preliminary_sizing:
  load_cases:
    - id: LC1
      description: |
        Peak Riveting Load: The condition where the tool applies the maximum required force (500 N) to the copper rivet at the end of the 4 mm stroke. This governs the structural integrity of the toggle linkage, pivot pins, and the C-frame.
      related_requirements:
        - R1
        - R2
        - R6
    - id: LC2
      description: |
        Maximum Operator Input: The condition where the operator applies the maximum allowable hand force (200 N) to the lever. This governs the bending strength of the lever arm and the ergonomic interface.
      related_requirements:
        - R7
        - R12
    - id: LC3
      description: |
        Cyclic Loading (Fatigue): Repeated operation over the 5-year service life (approx. 87,500 cycles). This governs the wear allowance for pivot bushings and the fatigue limit of the toggle links.
      related_requirements:
        - R10
    - id: LC4
      description: |
        Mounting Reaction: The reaction forces transmitted through the base plate to the wooden worktop during peak riveting. This governs the base plate thickness and bolt spacing.
      related_requirements:
        - R14
  sizing_results:
    - parameter_id: KDP1
      governing_case: LC1
      equation_or_model: |
        MA_total = F_rivet / F_input = (L_lever / L_pivot_arm) * (1 / tan(theta))
        where theta is the angle of the toggle links relative to the vertical axis at the point of closure.
      assumed_values:
        F_rivet: 500 N
        F_input: 100 N (Targeting 50% of limit for ergonomics)
        theta: 5 degrees (near toggle lock)
      result: "Total Mechanical Advantage required: 5:1"
      margin_or_factor_of_safety: "2.0 (relative to R7 limit of 200 N)"
    - parameter_id: KDP5
      governing_case: LC2
      equation_or_model: |
        sigma_max = (M * y) / I = (F_input * L_lever * c) / I
        where I is the area moment of inertia of the lever cross-section.
      assumed_values:
        F_input: 200 N
        L_lever: 550 mm
        Material: Steel S235 (Yield = 235 MPa)
      result: "Minimum Lever Section Modulus (W) = 1.17 cm^3"
      margin_or_factor_of_safety: "2.5 (Safety factor against yielding)"
    - parameter_id: B1
      governing_case: LC1
      equation_or_model: |
        tau = F_rivet / (2 * A_pin)
        d_pin = sqrt((2 * F_rivet) / (pi * tau_allow))
      assumed_values:
        F_rivet: 500 N
        tau_allow: 60 MPa (for mild steel pins in double shear)
      result: "Minimum Pivot Pin Diameter: 8 mm"
      margin_or_factor_of_safety: "4.0 (High factor to account for wear/LC3)"
    - parameter_id: B2
      governing_case: LC1
      equation_or_model: |
        delta = (F_rivet * H^3) / (3 * E * I_frame)
        Simplified as a cantilever beam deflection for the C-frame throat.
      assumed_values:
        F_rivet: 500 N
        H (Throat Depth): 150 mm
        E: 210 GPa
        Allowable delta: 0.1 mm (to maintain R2 stroke accuracy)
      result: "Minimum Frame Moment of Inertia: 2.68 cm^4"
      margin_or_factor_of_safety: "1.5"
    - parameter_id: B3
      governing_case: LC1
      equation_or_model: |
        sigma_comp = F_rivet / A_punch
      assumed_values:
        F_rivet: 500 N
        d_punch: 10 mm (standard shank for 3-5mm rivets)
      result: "Compressive Stress: 6.37 MPa"
      margin_or_factor_of_safety: ">10 (Governed by buckling or hardness KDP3 rather than pure compression)"
  assumptions:
    - The toggle linkage is designed to be near its "dead center" (lock) position at the 4 mm stroke completion to maximize force.
    - Pivot pins are in double shear within the linkage assembly.
    - The C-frame is constructed from standard steel plate or C-channel available in the workshop.
    - Friction losses in the pivots are estimated at 10% of the input energy.
    - The 500 N force is a static peak; dynamic effects from manual actuation are negligible.

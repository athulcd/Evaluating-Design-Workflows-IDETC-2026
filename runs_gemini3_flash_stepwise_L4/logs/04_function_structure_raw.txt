working_principles:
  principles:
    - id: WP1
      function_id: F1
      principle_name: Sliding Radius Support
      physical_effect_or_mechanism: |
        A sliding block mounted on a linear rail or T-slot, adjustable along the radial axis of the brake shoe. The block features a curved or V-shaped interface to support the shoe rim.
      key_parameters:
        - Adjustment range (150mm to 200mm radius)
        - Locking screw torque
        - Support surface width (min 100mm)
      advantages:
        - Continuously adjustable for any diameter within the range
        - Simple to manufacture using milling and drilling
      disadvantages:
        - Requires manual adjustment and locking for each batch
        - Potential for misalignment if the rail is not perpendicular to the punch
      risks:
        - Locking screw may vibrate loose over 70 cycles/day
      impacted_requirements:
        - R3
        - R4
        - R6

    - id: WP2
      function_id: F1
      principle_name: Interchangeable Radius Mandrels
      physical_effect_or_mechanism: |
        A set of fixed-radius support blocks (e.g., 250mm, 325mm, 400mm) that can be quickly swapped on a central mounting post.
      key_parameters:
        - Mandrel radius
        - Mounting pin diameter
      advantages:
        - Extremely stable support
        - Faster setup for standard sizes
      disadvantages:
        - Higher material cost for multiple mandrels
        - Storage required for unused mandrels
      risks:
        - Mandrels may be lost in a busy workshop environment
      impacted_requirements:
        - R3
        - R8
        - R13

    - id: WP3
      function_id: F2
      principle_name: Spring-Loaded Centering Pilot
      physical_effect_or_mechanism: |
        A small-diameter retractable pin located in the center of the riveting punch. The pin enters the rivet's tubular shank to align it before the forming pressure is applied.
      key_parameters:
        - Pilot pin diameter (2.5mm to 4.5mm)
        - Spring constant
        - Retraction depth
      advantages:
        - Ensures perfect concentricity between rivet and punch
        - Prevents rivet from tipping during the start of the stroke
      disadvantages:
        - More complex machining (internal bore in punch)
        - Small pins are prone to bending or breaking
      risks:
        - Pin fatigue failure over 5-year service life
      impacted_requirements:
        - R5
        - R10

    - id: WP4
      function_id: F2
      principle_name: Recessed Die Pocket
      physical_effect_or_mechanism: |
        The lower die features a shallow counterbore or spherical depression matching the rivet head geometry, acting as a nest.
      key_parameters:
        - Pocket depth
        - Pocket diameter (matching DIN 7338)
      advantages:
        - Very low cost (simple lathe operation)
        - No moving parts
      disadvantages:
        - Does not actively hold the rivet if the assembly is tilted
      risks:
        - Rivet may fall out during positioning of the heavy brake shoe
      impacted_requirements:
        - R5
        - R8

    - id: WP5
      function_id: F3
      principle_name: Class 1 Hand Lever
      physical_effect_or_mechanism: |
        A long steel bar rotating around a fixed fulcrum, providing mechanical advantage based on the ratio of the effort arm to the load arm.
      key_parameters:
        - Lever length (approx. 500-700mm)
        - Fulcrum pin diameter
      advantages:
        - Simple and intuitive operation
        - Easy to manufacture in-house
      disadvantages:
        - Requires significant vertical or horizontal clearance
        - May cause operator fatigue if the handle height is not ergonomic
      risks:
        - High bending moments on the fulcrum pin
      impacted_requirements:
        - R7
        - R12
        - R13

    - id: WP6
      function_id: F4
      principle_name: Toggle Linkage (Knee Lever)
      physical_effect_or_mechanism: |
        A two-bar linkage that approaches a straight line (180 degrees) at the end of the stroke, providing a theoretically infinite mechanical advantage at the point of rivet closure.
      key_parameters:
        - Link lengths
        - Transmission angle at end-of-stroke
      advantages:
        - Maximum force (500N+) delivered exactly when needed for deformation
        - Low input force required from the operator
      disadvantages:
        - Sensitive to assembly thickness variations
        - More pivot points to maintain and lubricate
      risks:
        - Mechanical jam if the assembly is thicker than 15mm
      impacted_requirements:
        - R1
        - R2
        - R7

    - id: WP7
      function_id: F4
      principle_name: Eccentric Cam Actuator
      physical_effect_or_mechanism: |
        A rotating circular cam with an offset center that pushes a follower (the punch) downwards.
      key_parameters:
        - Cam eccentricity (offset)
        - Cam profile radius
      advantages:
        - Very compact design
        - Precise control over the 3-4mm stroke
      disadvantages:
        - High surface contact stresses between cam and follower
        - Requires hardening of the cam surface
      risks:
        - Rapid wear if not properly lubricated in a workshop environment
      impacted_requirements:
        - R2
        - R10
        - R13

    - id: WP8
      function_id: F5
      principle_name: Compression Punch and Die
      physical_effect_or_mechanism: |
        Direct axial compression of the copper rivet between a hardened steel punch (upper) and die (lower) to create a flared or rolled head.
      key_parameters:
        - Tool steel grade (e.g., O1 or D2)
        - Surface finish of the forming face
      advantages:
        - Robust and reliable
        - Easy to replace if worn
      disadvantages:
        - Requires high peak force (500N)
      risks:
        - Brittle failure of the punch if over-hardened
      impacted_requirements:
        - R1
        - R5
        - R10

    - id: WP9
      function_id: F6
      principle_name: Mechanical Hard Stop
      physical_effect_or_mechanism: |
        A physical interference between the lever/linkage and the tool frame that prevents further movement once the 4mm stroke is reached.
      key_parameters:
        - Stop position adjustment
        - Contact area (to prevent mushrooming)
      advantages:
        - Clear tactile feedback to the operator
        - Ensures consistent rivet height
      disadvantages:
        - No visual indication for the operator before the stop is hit
      risks:
        - Repeated impact may damage the tool frame over time
      impacted_requirements:
        - R2
        - R12

    - id: WP10
      function_id: F7
      principle_name: Bolted Steel Base Plate
      physical_effect_or_mechanism: |
        A thick (10-15mm) steel plate with pre-drilled holes for M10 or M12 bolts to secure the tool to the wooden worktop.
      key_parameters:
        - Plate thickness
        - Bolt hole pattern
      advantages:
        - Maximum stability under the 500N load
        - Low cost and easy to manufacture
      disadvantages:
        - Requires drilling holes in the workshop's wooden worktop
      risks:
        - Wood may strip or compress over time, leading to tool wobble
      impacted_requirements:
        - R14
        - R8

  assumptions:
    - The workshop has the capability to heat-treat tool steel for the punch and die (WP8).
    - The wooden worktop is at least 30mm thick to provide a secure anchor for WP10.
    - Standard DIN 7338 rivets are semi-tubular, making the 500N force sufficient for closure.

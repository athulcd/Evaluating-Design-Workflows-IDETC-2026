black_box:
  overall_function: |
    Transform separate brake components (shoe and lining), fasteners (rivets), and human mechanical energy into a permanently joined brake shoe assembly through controlled plastic deformation of the rivets.
  flows:
    inputs:
      - id: IN1
        type: material
        name: Brake shoe and friction lining
        description: Pre-drilled components with diameters 250-400 mm and thickness up to 15 mm.
      - id: IN2
        type: material
        name: DIN 7338 copper rivets
        description: Tubular or semi-tubular copper rivets with 3-5 mm shank diameter.
      - id: IN3
        type: energy
        name: Human actuation force
        description: Manual input force from the operator (Hand < 200 N or Foot < 400 N).
      - id: IN4
        type: signal
        name: Alignment/Positioning signal
        description: Visual and tactile information used by the operator to align the rivet with the punch and die.
    outputs:
      - id: OUT1
        type: material
        name: Assembled brake shoe
        description: The friction lining permanently attached to the brake shoe via set rivets.
      - id: OUT2
        type: energy
        name: Dissipated energy
        description: Thermal energy from rivet deformation and acoustic energy (noise) during the riveting stroke.
      - id: OUT3
        type: signal
        name: Completion feedback
        description: Tactile resistance or visual confirmation indicating the 3-4 mm stroke is complete and the rivet is set.
  external_interactions:
    - id: EI1
      description: Mechanical mounting interface with a wooden worktop to resist the 500 N riveting force.
      related_requirements:
        - R1
        - R14
    - id: EI2
      description: Ergonomic interaction with a single operator during a 70-rivet daily workload.
      related_requirements:
        - R7
        - R10
        - R12
    - id: EI3
      description: Exposure to workshop environment (dust, lubricants, and varying temperatures).
      related_requirements:
        - R10
  assumptions:
    - The tool is purely mechanical and does not require external power sources (pneumatic, hydraulic, or electric).
    - The brake shoes and linings are provided to the tool already cleaned and pre-drilled.
    - The operator has sufficient physical capability to provide the required actuation force repeatedly.
  open_questions:
    - Is there a specific preference for hand-operated versus foot-operated actuation?
    - What are the specific dimensions or bolt patterns required for the wooden worktop mounting?
    - Are there specific noise level limits for the workshop environment?
  traceability:
    derived_from_requirements:
      - R1
      - R2
      - R3
      - R5
      - R6
      - R7
      - R10
      - R12
      - R14

embodiment_layouts:
  layout:
    - id: EL1
      module_breakdown:
        - module_id: M1
          name: Main Frame and Mounting Base
          responsibilities:
            - Provide structural rigidity for riveting forces (min 500 N)
            - Interface with workshop bench or vice
            - Support the lever pivot and riveting ram guide
          allocated_functions:
            - F9 (ENSURE stability on table)
            - F10 (ENSURE safety)
        - module_id: M2
          name: Actuation and Amplification Assembly
          responsibilities:
            - Accept manual input from operator (hand or foot)
            - Amplify input force to required riveting force via lever mechanism
            - Transfer motion to the riveting ram
          allocated_functions:
            - F4 (ACCEPT actuating force)
            - F5 (AMPLIFY force)
        - module_id: M3
          name: Riveting Head and Stroke Control
          responsibilities:
            - Form the rivet closure head
            - Provide adjustable hard stop for precise closure (approx. 4 mm)
            - Guide the linear motion of the forming tool
          allocated_functions:
            - F6 (CONTROL stroke and force)
            - F7 (FORM rivet head)
            - F3 (ALIGN work zone)
        - module_id: M4
          name: Workpiece Support Fixture
          responsibilities:
            - Support the brake shoe and lining assembly
            - Accommodate various shoe diameters (250-400 mm) and widths (80-400 mm)
            - Allow repositioning for multiple rivets
          allocated_functions:
            - F1 (Prepare and CLAMP workpiece)
            - F2 (INSERT and POSITION rivet)
            - F8 (ENABLE repositioning for next rivet)
      interfaces:
        - id: IF1
          between: [M1, M2]
          type: [mechanical]
          description: Lever pivot interface
          constraints:
            - Must withstand reaction forces from 500 N riveting load
            - Low friction bearing/bushing required for 5-year service life
        - id: IF2
          between: [M2, M3]
          type: [mechanical]
          description: Linkage between lever and riveting ram
          constraints:
            - Must convert angular lever motion to linear ram motion
            - Minimize side-loading on the ram guide
        - id: IF3
          between: [M3, M4]
          type: [mechanical]
          description: Working zone interface (Tooling to Workpiece)
          constraints:
            - Alignment between upper forming tool and lower support anvil
            - Clearance for 45 mm maximum stack thickness
        - id: IF4
          between: [M2, human]
          type: [human]
          description: Operator handle/pedal interface
          constraints:
            - Hand force <= 200 N or Foot force <= 400 N
            - Ergonomic grip/surface for workshop environment (oil/dust)
      key_design_parameters:
        - id: KDP1
          name: Lever Mechanical Advantage Ratio
          unit: "ratio"
          target_or_range: "2.5:1 to 3.5:1"
          linked_requirements: [R8, R17]
        - id: KDP2
          name: Throat Depth (Frame Clearance)
          unit: "mm"
          target_or_range: "> 200 mm"
          linked_requirements: [R3]
        - id: KDP3
          name: Adjustable Stop Resolution
          unit: "mm/turn"
          target_or_range: "1.0 - 1.5 mm (Fine thread)"
          linked_requirements: [R9]
        - id: KDP4
          name: Vertical Working Opening
          unit: "mm"
          target_or_range: ">= 50 mm"
          linked_requirements: [R5]
      risks_and_mitigations:
        - id: RM1
          risk: Frame deflection under 500 N load leads to inconsistent rivet closure.
          mitigation: Perform FEA on C-frame; use ribbed cast iron or heavy-gauge welded steel.
        - id: RM2
          risk: Pinch hazard between lever and frame during high-force stroke.
          mitigation: Design mechanical stops and provide protective shrouding or safe-clearance zones.
        - id: RM3
          risk: Adjustment drift of the stroke stop due to vibration/repeated impact.
          mitigation: Use a secondary lock-nut or detent mechanism on the adjustment screw.
  assumptions:
    - The "400 mm width" requirement (R3) refers to the total width of the shoe, but the riveting line is typically accessible from the side; throat depth may not need to be the full 400 mm.
    - Standard DIN 7338 rivets are used as the baseline for forming tool geometry.
  open_questions:

    - Does the "wood worktop" (R12) require a specific clamping plate to prevent damage to the bench?

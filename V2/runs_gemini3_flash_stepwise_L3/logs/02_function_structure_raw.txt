    - id: WP1
      function_id: F1
      principle_name: Adjustable Radius Support (Prism/V-block)
      physical_effect_or_mechanism: |
        Two adjustable support points or a V-shaped block that centers the curved brake shoe relative to the riveting axis. 
        Sliding mechanisms allow the supports to move inward or outward to accommodate different diameters.
      key_parameters:
        - Support span (distance between contact points)
        - Adjustment range (250-400 mm diameter)
        - Lateral guide width (up to 400 mm)
      advantages:
        - Highly versatile for various shoe sizes
        - Simple mechanical adjustment
      disadvantages:
        - Requires manual setup for each shoe size
        - Potential for misalignment if not locked properly
      risks:
        - Slippage of the shoe during high-force application if friction is low
      impacted_requirements:
        - R2
        - R3
        - R4

    - id: WP2
      function_id: F1
      principle_name: Interchangeable Radius Bolsters
      physical_effect_or_mechanism: |
        A set of fixed-radius support blocks (bolsters) that match specific brake shoe curvatures. 
        The operator swaps the bolster based on the shoe being serviced.
      key_parameters:
        - Bolster radius
        - Mounting interface (quick-change)
      advantages:
        - Very stable and precise support
        - No adjustment errors once the correct part is selected
      disadvantages:
        - Requires storage for multiple bolsters
        - Higher manufacturing cost for the set
      risks:
        - Operator using the wrong bolster size, leading to shoe deformation
      impacted_requirements:
        - R2
        - R20

    - id: WP3
      function_id: F2
      principle_name: Spring-Loaded Centering Sleeve
      physical_effect_or_mechanism: |
        A hollow sleeve surrounding the riveting punch. The sleeve is spring-biased to extend forward, 
        holding the rivet and lining in alignment with the shoe hole before the punch makes contact.
      key_parameters:
        - Spring constant
        - Sleeve inner diameter (3-5 mm)
      advantages:
        - Hands-free holding of the rivet during the stroke
        - Improves safety by keeping fingers away from the pinch point
      disadvantages:
        - Adds complexity to the punch assembly
        - Potential for the sleeve to jam with workshop dust
      risks:
        - Spring fatigue over the 5-year service life
      impacted_requirements:
        - R7
        - R18

    - id: WP4
      function_id: F3
      principle_name: Toggle Lever (Knee-Joint) Mechanism
      physical_effect_or_mechanism: |
        A linkage system where two bars move toward a straight-line (dead center) position. 
        The mechanical advantage approaches infinity as the links align, providing maximum force at the end of the stroke.
      key_parameters:
        - Link lengths
        - Input lever arm length
        - Transmission angle
      advantages:
        - Ideal force profile for riveting (highest force at the end of deformation)
        - High amplification with compact size
      disadvantages:
        - Force drops off rapidly if the rivet is not positioned at the correct stroke height
      risks:
        - High internal stresses on pins and joints
      impacted_requirements:
        - R8
        - R17

    - id: WP5
      function_id: F3
      principle_name: Eccentric Cam Actuation
      physical_effect_or_mechanism: |
        A rotating cam with an offset profile that pushes a follower (the punch). 
        The cam profile can be designed to provide a specific force-displacement curve.
      key_parameters:
        - Cam eccentricity
        - Cam profile geometry
        - Handle length
      advantages:
        - Very robust and simple (few moving parts)
        - Easy to achieve a smooth stroke
      disadvantages:
        - High friction at the cam-follower interface
        - Limited stroke length compared to levers
      risks:
        - Wear on the cam surface reducing the final riveting force
      impacted_requirements:
        - R8
        - R17
        - R13

    - id: WP6
      function_id: F4
      principle_name: Axial Compression (Steady Press)
      physical_effect_or_mechanism: |
        The rivet head is formed by purely axial pressure between a shaped punch and a stationary anvil. 
        The material flows plastically to fill the hole and form the closure head.
      key_parameters:
        - Punch tip geometry (cup or flat)
        - Final stroke depth
      advantages:
        - Quiet operation
        - Consistent head shape
      disadvantages:
        - Requires very high peak forces (>= 500 N)
      risks:
        - Over-pressing can crack the brittle brake lining
      impacted_requirements:
        - R1
        - R9

    - id: WP7
      function_id: F5
      principle_name: Adjustable Mechanical Hard Stop
      physical_effect_or_mechanism: |
        A physical bolt or collar that limits the travel of the lever or punch. 
        The operator feels a sudden increase in resistance when the target depth is reached.
      key_parameters:
        - Stop position (adjustable for 4 mm closure)
      advantages:
        - Highly reliable tactile feedback
        - Prevents over-riveting
      disadvantages:
        - Must be adjusted if rivet sizes or material thicknesses change
      risks:
        - Stop loosening over time due to repeated impacts
      impacted_requirements:
        - R9
        - R11

  selected_concept_id: CV1
  selection_rationale: |
    CV1 (Universal Workshop Lever) is selected as the preferred concept. While CV2 offers 
    superior joint quality through matched bolsters, CV1 better balances the overall 
    requirements of a workshop environment. Specifically, CV1 satisfies the portability 
    wish (R14) via the vice-clamping shank (WP8) and is significantly more likely to 
    stay within the SwFr 400 cost constraint (R20). The toggle lever (WP4) provides 
    an ideal force profile for riveting, and the adjustable V-block (WP1) ensures 
    the tool can handle the full range of specified brake shoe diameters without 
    requiring the manufacture and storage of additional parts.
  sensitivity_analysis_notes: |
    The selection is sensitive to the weighting of 'Quality' vs 'Cost/Portability'. 
    If the requirement for joint precision is increased (e.g., for high-performance 
    racing brakes), CV2 would become the stronger candidate. However, for standard 
    internal drum brakes in a general workshop, the versatility and lower cost of 
    CV1 are decisive. If the workshop does not have a vice, CV1's stability score 
    would drop significantly, potentially shifting the preference to a bolted base.
  assumptions:
    - A standard workshop vice is available for CV1.
    - The toggle lever mechanism in CV1 can be manufactured using standard steel profiles to keep costs low.
    - The 500 N force requirement refers to the output force at the rivet, which both mechanisms can achieve.

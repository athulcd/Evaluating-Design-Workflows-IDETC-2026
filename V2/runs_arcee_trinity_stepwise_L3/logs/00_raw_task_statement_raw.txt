The tool must be hand or foot-operated, ergonomic, reliable, maintenance-free, compact, and easy to transport. It should handle copper rivets (DIN 7338, 3-5 mm shank diameter) with a riveting force of ~500 N and a working stroke of ~3-4 mm. The tool must accommodate brake shoes (aluminum alloy, up to 2 kg, 100 mm width) with linings (friction material, up to 200 g) and a max assembled thickness of 15 mm. It must work on brake drums with an inside diameter of 250-400 mm, provide 10 mm clearance from the rivet to the brake shoe rib, and be manufacturable in-house for under 500 CHF/unit (target 400 CHF) within 3-4 months.

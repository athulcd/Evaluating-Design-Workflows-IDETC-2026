working_principles:
  principles:
    - id: WP1
      function_id: F1
      principle_name: Manual Screw Clamp
      physical_effect_or_mechanism: Friction and Wedge (Screw Thread)
      key_parameters:
        - Thread pitch
        - Handle diameter
        - Clamping surface area
      advantages:
        - High clamping force capability
        - Infinitely adjustable for different shoe thicknesses (R4)
        - Simple and robust construction
      disadvantages:
        - Slow operation (low productivity R10)
        - Requires manual effort for every rivet cycle
      risks:
        - Over-tightening could damage Al-alloy brake shoes
      impacted_requirements:
        - R3
        - R4
        - R10

    - id: WP2
      function_id: F1
      principle_name: Over-Center Toggle Clamp
      physical_effect_or_mechanism: Linkage Kinematics (Singularity)
      key_parameters:
        - Link lengths
        - Pivot friction
      advantages:
        - Very fast clamping/unclamping (supports R10)
        - Repeatable clamping force
      disadvantages:
        - Limited adjustment range for varying thicknesses
        - More complex assembly than a screw
      risks:
        - Sudden release could cause minor injury
      impacted_requirements:
        - R4
        - R10
        - R18

    - id: WP3
      function_id: F2
      principle_name: Manual Rivet Insertion
      physical_effect_or_mechanism: Human Manipulation
      key_parameters:
        - Operator dexterity
      advantages:
        - Zero additional tool cost (R20)
        - No mechanical complexity
      disadvantages:
        - Slowest method
        - Risk of dropping rivets in workshop environment
      risks:
        - Misalignment if not seated properly
      impacted_requirements:
        - R7
        - R10
        - R20

    - id: WP4
      function_id: F2
      principle_name: Magnetic Rivet Holder
      physical_effect_or_mechanism: Magnetism
      key_parameters:
        - Magnetic flux density
        - Rivet material (must be ferromagnetic)
      advantages:
        - Holds rivet in place during positioning
        - Prevents rivets from falling out
      disadvantages:
        - Only works with steel rivets (DIN 7338 often steel, but check)
        - Can attract workshop metal dust/debris
      risks:
        - Magnet degradation over time (R13)
      impacted_requirements:
        - R7
        - R15

    - id: WP5
      function_id: F3
      principle_name: Mechanical Indexing Pin
      physical_effect_or_mechanism: Physical Interference
      key_parameters:
        - Pin diameter
        - Hole tolerance
      advantages:
        - High precision alignment (R15)
        - Repeatable results across multiple rivets
      disadvantages:
        - Requires pin to match rivet hole size
        - Adds a step to the process
      risks:
        - Pin wear affecting accuracy
      impacted_requirements:
        - R6
        - R15

    - id: WP6
      function_id: F4
      principle_name: Long-Arm Hand Lever
      physical_effect_or_mechanism: Leverage (Torque Balance)
      key_parameters:
        - Lever length
        - Pivot point location
      advantages:
        - Simple to manufacture (R20)
        - Direct feedback to operator
      disadvantages:
        - Requires significant space for swing
        - Force limited by operator arm strength (R17)
      risks:
        - Operator fatigue over 70 rivets/day
      impacted_requirements:
        - R11
        - R17
        - R20

    - id: WP7
      function_id: F4
      principle_name: Foot Pedal Actuation
      physical_effect_or_mechanism: Leverage and Cable/Rod Transmission
      key_parameters:
        - Pedal travel
        - Transmission ratio
      advantages:
        - Leaves both hands free for workpiece positioning (R11)
        - Higher force capability (400N vs 200N hand force)
      disadvantages:
        - More complex mechanical linkage
        - Less portable (R14)
      risks:
        - Stability issues if not bolted down
      impacted_requirements:
        - R11
        - R14
        - R17

    - id: WP8
      function_id: F5
      principle_name: Toggle Linkage (Knee Joint)
      physical_effect_or_mechanism: Geometric Force Amplification
      key_parameters:
        - Angle of links near closure
        - Pivot stiffness
      advantages:
        - Extremely high force at the end of the stroke (ideal for R8)
        - Compact design for high amplification
      disadvantages:
        - Non-linear force profile
        - Sensitive to setup height
      risks:
        - High stress on pivot pins
      impacted_requirements:
        - R8
        - R17

    - id: WP9
      function_id: F6
      principle_name: Adjustable Threaded Hard Stop
      physical_effect_or_mechanism: Physical Displacement Limit
      key_parameters:
        - Thread pitch (fine thread preferred)
      advantages:
        - Precise control of rivet head closure (R9)
        - Easy to adjust for different material stacks
      disadvantages:
        - Can be accidentally moved if not locked
      risks:
        - Inconsistent closure if stop is not reached by operator
      impacted_requirements:
        - R9
        - R15

    - id: WP10
      function_id: F7
      principle_name: Cold Pressing (Steady Pressure)
      physical_effect_or_mechanism: Plastic Deformation
      key_parameters:
        - Die geometry
        - Applied force
      advantages:
        - Quiet operation
        - High quality, consistent head shape (R1)
      disadvantages:
        - Requires high peak force (R8)
      risks:
        - Material work-hardening
      impacted_requirements:
        - R1
        - R8
        - R9

    - id: WP11
      function_id: F9
      principle_name: Vice-Mounting Flange
      physical_effect_or_mechanism: Friction (Clamping)
      key_parameters:
        - Flange thickness
        - Surface finish
      advantages:
        - Extremely portable (R14)
        - Uses existing workshop equipment (vice)
      disadvantages:
        - Stability depends on the quality of the vice
      risks:
        - Tool slipping if vice is oily
      impacted_requirements:
        - R12
        - R14

    - id: WP12
      function_id: F10
      principle_name: Safety Clearance Design
      physical_effect_or_mechanism: Geometric Separation
      key_parameters:
        - Minimum gap distance
      advantages:
        - No moving parts to fail
        - Zero cost
      disadvantages:
        - Does not protect against all finger positions
      risks:
        - Operator bypass
      impacted_requirements:
        - R18

  assumptions:
    - Rivets used are standard DIN 7338 as per the case context.
    - The workshop vice is a standard resource available to the mechanic.
    - The brake shoe material (Al-alloy) requires controlled pressure to avoid cracking.

  open_questions:
    - Is the foot pedal option preferred by mechanics for high-volume (70 rivets/day) work?
    - Are the rivets typically steel, copper, or aluminum (affects magnetic principle WP4)?

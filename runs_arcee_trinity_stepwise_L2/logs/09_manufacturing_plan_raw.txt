manufacturing_plan:
  module_plans:
    - module_id: M1
      material_candidates:
        - material: Mild steel (e.g., ASTM A36)
          rationale: High stiffness, good weldability, low cost, easy to machine; suitable for base/frame under static loads.
          process_candidates:
            - process: Welding + machining
              rationale: Allows complex shapes, good strength, workshop-friendly.
            - process: Casting (gray iron)
              rationale: High damping, good stability, but higher tooling cost and longer lead time.
          risks:
            - Welding distortion if not controlled
            - Castings may have porosity or require machining allowances
          critical_characteristics:
            - Stiffness to resist deflection under 500 N load
            - Surface flatness for mounting
      - material: Aluminum alloy (e.g., 6061-T6)
        rationale: Lightweight, corrosion resistant, easier to machine; beneficial for portability (EC5).
        process_candidates:
          - process: CNC machining
            rationale: High precision, good surface finish, but higher cost.
          - process: Extrusion + machining
            rationale: Cost-effective for long members, but limited to simple cross-sections.
        risks:
          - Lower stiffness than steel; may require larger sections
          - Anodizing adds cost and lead time
        critical_characteristics:
          - Deflection under load
          - Surface finish for clamping

    - module_id: M2
      material_candidates:
        - material: Hardened steel (e.g., 4140)
          rationale: Wear resistance for repeated clamping, high strength.
          process_candidates:
            - process: Machining + heat treatment
              rationale: Precise geometry, durable.
            - process: Investment casting + machining
              rationale: Complex shapes possible, but higher cost.
          risks:
            - Heat treatment distortion
            - Higher cost than mild steel
          critical_characteristics:
            - Wear resistance at contact points
            - Dimensional stability
      - material: Polymer (e.g., glass-filled nylon)
        rationale: Low cost, corrosion resistant, self-lubricating.
        process_candidates:
          - process: Injection molding
            rationale: High volume, complex shapes, but tooling cost.
          - process: CNC machining (for prototypes)
            rationale: No tooling, but higher per-part cost.
        risks:
          - Lower strength than metal
          - Creep under sustained load
        critical_characteristics:
          - Load-bearing capacity
          - Coefficient of friction

    - module_id: M3
      material_candidates:
        - material: Hardened steel (e.g., 1095)
          rationale: Wear resistance for rivet guide, precise bore.
          process_candidates:
            - process: Machining + heat treatment
              rationale: High precision, durability.
            - process: EDM (for complex shapes)
              rationale: Tight tolerances, but higher cost.
          risks:
            - Alignment critical; any bore error causes rivet misfeed
          critical_characteristics:
            - Bore diameter tolerance (for 3-5 mm rivets)
            - Surface finish to prevent galling

    - module_id: M4
      material_candidates:
        - material: Aluminum alloy (e.g., 6063-T5)
          rationale: Lightweight, easy to form, good for ergonomic shaping.
          process_candidates:
            - process: Extrusion + bending + machining
              rationale: Cost-effective for long handles, good strength-to-weight.
            - process: Casting (for complex shapes)
              rationale: Allows ergonomic grips, but higher cost.
          risks:
            - Fatigue under cyclic loading
            - Surface treatment needed for grip
          critical_characteristics:
            - Fatigue strength
            - Surface texture for grip

    - module_id: M5
      material_candidates:
        - material: Carbon steel (e.g., 1020)
          rationale: High strength, good weldability, low cost.
          process_candidates:
            - process: Welding + machining
              rationale: Allows custom linkage geometry, robust joints.
            - process: Forging (for high-stress parts)
              rationale: Highest strength, but higher cost and tooling.
          risks:
            - Stress concentrations at welds or bends
            - Wear at pivot points
          critical_characteristics:
            - Yield strength under amplified load
            - Pivot bearing life

    - module_id: M6
      material_candidates:
        - material: Tool steel (e.g., D2 or S7)
          rationale: High hardness, wear resistance for forming heads.
          process_candidates:
            - process: Machining + heat treatment
              rationale: Precise geometry, long life.
            - process: Powder metallurgy (for complex shapes)
              rationale: Near-net shape, but higher cost.
          risks:
            - Brittleness if not properly heat treated
            - Alignment critical for consistent closure
          critical_characteristics:
            - Hardness (Rockwell C 55-60)
            - Surface finish for smooth forming

    - module_id: M7
      material_candidates:
        - material: Stainless steel (e.g., 303)
          rationale: Corrosion resistance, good machinability, lockable via threads.
          process_candidates:
            - process: CNC machining
              rationale: High precision, reliable threads.
            - process: Threaded insert in base material
              rationale: Allows field replacement, but adds cost.
          risks:
            - Thread wear over time
            - Adjustment drift if not locked
          critical_characteristics:
            - Thread pitch accuracy
            - Locking mechanism reliability

    - module_id: M8
      material_candidates:
        - material: Polymer (e.g., ABS or polycarbonate)
          rationale: Impact resistance, easy to mold, good for guards.
          process_candidates:
            - process: Injection molding
              rationale: Cost-effective for guards, complex shapes.
            - process: Thermoforming (for flat guards)
              rationale: Low cost, but limited to simple shapes.
          risks:
            - UV degradation if used outdoors
            - Sharp edges if not properly finished
          critical_characteristics:
            - Impact strength
            - Clarity (if visual inspection needed)

    - module_id: M9
      material_candidates:
        - material: Stainless steel (e.g., 304)
          rationale: Corrosion resistance, good for gauges.
          process_candidates:
            - process: Stamping + machining
              rationale: Cost-effective for flat gauges.
            - process: Laser cutting + etching
              rationale: High precision, but higher cost.
          risks:
            - Calibration drift if not hardened
          critical_characteristics:
            - Dimensional accuracy
            - Readability of markings

  assumptions:
    - Materials and processes are selected for workshop production with typical resources (no specialized tooling beyond standard machining/welding).
    - Cost target (SwFr 400) favors mild steel, aluminum, and polymer over exotic alloys or processes.
    - Lead time target (3 months) avoids long-lead items like custom castings or complex tooling.
    - Safety-critical items (M6 forming heads, M7 stop) use hardened/tool steels for durability and reliability.
    - Ergonomic and portability requirements (EC5) favor aluminum and polymer where strength allows.

  open_questions:

    - Verify if any regulatory or workshop safety standards require specific guard materials or certifications.
    - Determine if any parts (e.g., M2 fixture) can be outsourced to reduce in-house machining time.
    - Clarify if adjustable stop (M7) needs field calibration capability or if it's set at factory only.
